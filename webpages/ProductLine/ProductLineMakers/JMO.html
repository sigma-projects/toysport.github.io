JMO   (Japan Mobile Operations)

1966 Japan Mobil Operation Corporation was founded in Aoyama, Tokyo, Japan as a performance automotive service company. Their emphasis was on building race cars and participating in the major race circuits in Japan. JMO also supplied racing tire service and racing spark plugs to the drivers. After several years in
Tokyo, JMO moved to Sapporo in Northern Japan closer to the manufactures performance test track.

1978 JMO opened their United States division in Fullerton, California as a total service facility for import automobiles, and technical service facility for Nissan Racing.  They also received an appointment from Toyota Racing Development  (Japan)  as their technical service facility.

1995 JMO developed their own performance torque converter line, and now have more than 450 kinds of performance torque converters for use in gas, diesel, propane and electric vehicles for land and marine use.
